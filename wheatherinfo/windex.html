How Weather Impacts Crop-Yield Selection

Rainfall & Soil Moisture

Adequate rainfall and soil moisture are vital for crop germination, root development and growth. Too little rain can result in drought stress, while excessive rainfall may cause waterlogging and nutrient leaching — both impacting yield potential.

Temperature & Heat Stress

Temperature influences crop metabolism, photosynthesis and flowering. Heat waves or cold snaps during critical growth stages can significantly reduce yield. Studies show temperature is among the most influential factors for yield prediction. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Humidity, Wind & Evapotranspiration

Humidity and wind affect evaporation rates and plant water loss, impacting how much water the crop actually uses versus what is available. Understanding these metrics helps in irrigation scheduling and crop selection for your region.

Recommended Actions for Farmers

Check the forecast regularly

Use our dashboard to monitor upcoming weather events (rain, heat, storms). Prepare irrigation, cover crops or adjust field activities accordingly to avoid stress on your crops.

Choose crops suitable for your micro-climate

Based on your field’s typical weather and soil, pick varieties that handle the prevalent conditions (e.g., heat-tolerant, drought-resistant). Weather-integrated crop-yield models use such data for better recommendations.

Time planting for favourable conditions

Plant when conditions are optimal (soil moisture, moderate temperature) rather than under extreme weather. This improves establishment, growth and final yield.

Use weather data to schedule irrigation & inputs

Align fertiliser, irrigation and pest-control with upcoming weather (e.g., apply fertiliser when rain is unlikely). This leads to better efficiency and avoids loss of inputs through runoff or heat stress.
